Interesting Facts about Camel:

  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.

Interesting Facts about chameleon:

  • Chameleons are best known for their ability to change colors easily.
  • Chameleons range in length from 1 inch (2.5 centimeters) to 26.8 inches (68 centimeters)
  • Chameleons have long, slim legs, with four feet.

Interesting Facts about Flying-cicada:

  • Cicadas have prominent eyes set wide apart, short antennae, and membranous front wings.
  • The superfamily is divided into two families, Tettigarctidae, with two species in Australia, and Cicadidae, with more than 3,000 species described from around the world; many species remain undescribed.
  • One genus, the periodical cicadas spend most of their lives as underground nymphs, emerging only after 13 or 17 years.
